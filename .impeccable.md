## Design Context

### Users
PatientConnect360 is a shared home-health portal for four connected audiences: patients, caregivers/MPOAs, clinicians, and agency administrators. The visual system should be balanced across roles rather than optimized for only one persona.

Usage context varies by role:
- patients and families need clear, reassuring access to schedules, records, updates, and care coordination from phones and laptops
- clinicians need fast, legible workflow surfaces during active care delivery and documentation
- administrators need confident operational oversight across scheduling, messaging, compliance, and agency health

The product's job is to create continuity of care after discharge by making home-health communication, scheduling, records, and recovery visibility feel unified rather than fragmented.

### Brand Personality
Brand personality: bold, modern, and confident.

The interface should feel premium and assured without becoming cold, theatrical, or consumer-playful. It should communicate that this is serious healthcare software with strong taste, strong structure, and strong clarity.

### Aesthetic Direction
Direction: luxury healthcare for a balanced multi-role care ecosystem.

Visual intent:
- premium and composed rather than sterile
- modern and memorable rather than generic SaaS
- polished and intentional rather than flashy
- credible for healthcare while still feeling distinct and elevated

Anti-references:
- avoid bland generic SaaS dashboard patterns
- avoid cold hospital portal aesthetics
- avoid gaming UI / cyberpunk theatrics
- avoid playful consumer-app energy

Accessibility and interface constraints:
- maintain WCAG AA contrast as a baseline
- prioritize large touch targets for real-world multi-device use

### Design Principles
1. Design for continuity across roles: the system should feel like one product serving different moments of care, not disconnected mini-apps.
2. Express confidence through precision: hierarchy, spacing, copy, and interaction states should feel assured and deliberate.
3. Deliver premium healthcare, not luxury fashion: refinement should increase trust and clarity, never obscure function.
4. Differentiate through structure and materials, not gimmicks: avoid generic SaaS layouts while also avoiding sci-fi theatrics or decorative excess.
5. Optimize for real use in care environments: large targets, strong contrast, mobile readiness, and role-appropriate density are mandatory.
